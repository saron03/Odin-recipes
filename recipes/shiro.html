Shiro Wot Recipe

Description

Shiro wot is a traditional Ethiopian dish made from powdered chickpeas or broad beans seasoned with spices. It's typically cooked with water and oil, forming a thick, flavorful stew often served with injera, a type of sourdough flatbread. Simple yet rich in flavor, shiro wot is a staple in Ethiopian cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup shiro powder (chickpea or broad bean flour)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon berbere (Ethiopian spice blend)
  • 3 cups water
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a pot, add chopped onions and minced garlic, and onion until soft.
  2. Stir in berbere and cook for a few minutes.
  3. Gradually add shiro powder, stirring constantly to avoid lumps.
  4. Pour in water slowly, stirring continuously until smooth.
  5. Reduce heat and simmer for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until thickened.
  6. Add salt to taste and serve with injera.
